What good care looks like

How Your Care Should Work

After a crash, a good care provider does more than treat pain. Here's what a proper recovery process looks like — from day one to full recovery.

🔍

Precise Injury Assessment

Your provider should identify exactly what was injured — not just treat vague pain. This means a thorough evaluation, and imaging if needed, to understand what the crash actually did to your body.

🌊

Gentle, Progressive Relief

Good crash care starts gently. Adjustments, soft tissue work, and stretching reduce stiffness and restore movement without overwhelming your body in its injured state.

💪

Active Recovery

Real healing goes beyond passive treatment. Guided exercises, traction, and progressive rehab rebuild strength and prevent re-injury — getting you back to how you felt before the crash.

📋

Full Coordination

When needed, your provider should coordinate with specialists, order imaging, and communicate with your insurance and legal team. You shouldn't have to manage all of that alone.

Know your choices

What Options Do You Have?

Washington State gives you the right to choose your own care. Here's a quick look at the most common paths people take after an auto accident.

🦴

Chiropractic Care

The most common first step for soft tissue injuries, whiplash, and spinal issues. Covered by PIP in Washington with no referral needed. Often the fastest path to pain relief.

Great for whiplash PIP covered No referral needed Can order imaging Can send referrals Not for fractures
🏃

Physical Therapy

Focused on rebuilding strength and restoring function. Best used alongside chiropractic care or as a follow-up. Typically requires a physician referral.

Great for recovery Long-term function Usually needs referral
🩺

Medical Doctor / Urgent Care

Great for same-day documentation, imaging orders, and specialist referrals. Gets your injury on the record from day one — important for your insurance claim.

Good documentation Can order imaging Appointments can lag
🔬

Specialist Care

For complex injuries — disc herniation, nerve damage, orthopedic issues. Usually accessed via referral from your primary provider.

Complex cases Needs referral Longer wait times
🚑

Emergency Room

For serious symptoms: numbness in limbs, severe head pain, suspected fractures, or loss of consciousness. Don't wait — go immediately.

Immediate care For serious symptoms Not for ongoing care
⚖️

Personal Injury Attorney

For significant injuries, a PI attorney protects your rights and ensures the full value of your claim is recovered. Most work on contingency — no upfront cost.

No upfront cost Protects your rights For significant injuries
💆

Massage Therapy

Highly effective for soft tissue tension, muscle spasm, and stress from the trauma of a crash. Often used alongside chiropractic care to speed recovery and reduce pain between appointments.

Soft tissue relief Reduces muscle tension PIP may cover Not for structural injuries
🧠

Mental Health Care

Crashes are traumatic events. Anxiety, PTSD, sleep disruption, and depression are real and common after an accident — and often overlooked. A therapist or counselor can be a critical part of full recovery.

Addresses trauma & PTSD Often PIP covered Frequently overlooked

Common questions

Common Questions After a Crash

The questions we hear most — answered plainly, without the runaround.

Do I need care even if I feel fine?

Yes — and this is the most important thing to understand. Whiplash, concussions, and soft tissue damage often don't appear immediately. Adrenaline masks pain at the scene. Many people feel fine for 24–72 hours and then can barely move. Getting evaluated early catches injuries before they worsen and creates a medical record that matters for your claim.

How soon should I start treatment?

As soon as possible — ideally within the first week. Early treatment reduces pain, improves healing outcomes, and documents your injuries for insurance purposes. The longer you wait, the harder it becomes to connect your symptoms to the crash.

Who pays for my treatment?

In Washington, your own PIP (Personal Injury Protection) insurance pays first — regardless of who caused the crash. Washington requires a minimum of $10,000 in PIP on every auto policy. Most providers who specialize in crash care bill PIP directly, meaning no out-of-pocket costs for you upfront.

How long does recovery take?

It depends on your injuries. Some people recover in a few weeks with minor soft tissue injuries. Others with disc damage or more serious trauma may need several months. A good provider sets realistic expectations early and adjusts your plan as you heal — not lock you into a rigid timeline.

Do I need a lawyer?

For minor crashes with minimal injuries, probably not. For anything significant — ongoing pain, time off work, extensive medical bills — consulting a personal injury attorney is worth it. Most work on contingency (they only get paid if you do), and they protect you from insurance adjusters who may try to minimize your claim.

What's the difference between PIP and the other driver's insurance?

PIP is your own coverage and pays immediately — no fault determination required. The at-fault driver's liability insurance covers damages beyond your PIP, but only after fault is established, which takes time. Use PIP first to start care right away, then pursue the at-fault claim separately.

Can soft tissue injuries be treated without surgery?

In most cases, yes. The majority of auto accident injuries — whiplash, muscle strain, ligament sprains — respond well to chiropractic care, physical therapy, and guided rehabilitation. Surgery is typically a last resort for structural damage like severe disc herniation.
